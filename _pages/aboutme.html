I am a PhD student studying cognitive psychology at the University of Illinois Urbana Champaign. I work in the Human Memory and Cognition lab, under the supervision of Dr. Aaron Benjamin. My work sits at the intersection of perception, metacognition, and decision-making, with a particular focus on how metacognitive control shapes behavior over time.

I use an integrative approach that combines behavioral experiments, psychophysics, eye-tracking, physiological measures, and computational modeling. Across projects, I am especially interested in understanding not just what decisions people make, but how they regulate their behavior in response to uncertainty, task demands, and internal feedback. To this end, I frequently employ modeling frameworks such as signal detection theory, drift-diffusion models, item response theory, and hierarchical Bayesian methods to formalize latent cognitive processes.
