--- layout: archive title: Hi, I'm Brad permalink: /aboutme/ author_profile: true classes: wide --- Interactive Research Focus I am a PhD student studying cognitive psychology at the University of Illinois Urbana Champaign.

My research focuses on

Human Perception and Attention
  • How different timing and pacing manipulations can affect performance in tasks that require constant attention for searching
  • How individuals perceive and interpret visual illusions, such as the Müller-Lyer and Ponzo illusion
Metacognition
  • How individuals monitor and evaluate their own attentional states during tasks
  • How individuals use metacognition evaluation to guide strategies of their decision-making process
Decision Making
  • How individuals decide where to look at and what to choose in a recognition task
  • How individuals decide who to trust when responding as a group

I use an integrative approach that involves

Behavioral Studies
  • I code studies in multiple languages, including Python (PsychoPy), Matlab (PsychToolBox), and Javascript (JsPsych).
Process Tracing Methods
  • I am familiar with running eye-tracking and EEG studies and analyzing data from those studies.
Computational Models
  • I use Drift-Diffusion Model, hierarchical model, and other latent variable models (e.g., Signal Detection Model) for analyzing and simulating data.
